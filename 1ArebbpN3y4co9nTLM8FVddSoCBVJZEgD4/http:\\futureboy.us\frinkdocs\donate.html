I've been working on Frink for a long time, as a labor of love to make something I really believe in. If you find Frink (or any of my other software) to be useful and want to support its development, you can do so in a few ways:

  • Tell others about Frink. The best way to do this is to show somebody else how to use Frink and make their life easier. Teach a kid how to use Frink and give them a head start and a love of science that will last them a lifetime.
  • Send compliments to Alan Eliasen. This doesn't cost you anything, and makes me happy.

    "I have been complimented many times and they always embarrass me; I always feel that they have not said enough."
    --Mark Twain

  • Better yet, send suggestions or bug reports to Alan Eliasen. This will help me to improve Frink and help it work better for everyone.
  • If you have algorithms or programs or data that would be useful in Frink, please send them to me. We can work together to transliterate them into Frink. Useful stuff can become part of the standard distribution.
