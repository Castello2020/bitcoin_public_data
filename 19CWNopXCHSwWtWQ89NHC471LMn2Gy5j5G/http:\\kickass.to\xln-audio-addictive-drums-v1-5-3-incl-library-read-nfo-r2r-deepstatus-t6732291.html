tell your DAW to scan in the Addictive Drums folder, then it'll find it. scan your original VST folder and it should appear

Firstly, thanks deepstatus.
Secondly, I have this fully working on Win7 Ultimate x64. Here are the full steps.
1.Uninstall previous versions of AD. Delete ALL the folders associated with it i.e mydocs>addictive drums and XLN Audio in program files
2.Mount the AD iso, copy the Addictive drums folder from the mounted iso to x:Program filesx86
3.Install setup.exe from the iso, ONLY install the bit version that your DAW uses (e.g Cubase, Mixcraft etc). In my case I have Mixcraft x86, so I checked VST x86 and installed to C:Programfilesx86VSTpluginsXLNAddictive drums
4.in x:Program filesx86Addictive Drums -> AD_Library_Placer.exe
5. Done! Now just make sure your DAW VST plugins folders are pointed to where you installed. In my case C:Programfilesx86VSTpluginsXLNAddictive drums
