What are Bitcoins?

Bitcoins are a completely digital currency. Its distribution happens over so called peer-to-peer connections. All transactions are carried out through a strong encryption and a high grade of anonymity. Every transaction is traceable but not the user behind these transactions.

The system’s advantage is, that no bank or state can have influence on the price and cash flow. That means: Bitcoins have no standing orders or borders, every user everywhere can create and transfer Bitcoins.

Bitcoins are limited to 21 million. Doesn’t sound much but draw attention to the fact, that every Bitcoin can be divided into 100 million parts. That means there are 21 quadrillion single transferable Bitcoin units!

Where do I get more Bitcoins?

There are different ways. You can buy Bitcoins – just use the many trading platforms like MtGox.com or bitcoin.de. Here you can trade Bitcoins into your preferred currency.

The second, more complex method is the so called mining – the creation of your own Bitcoins. To be a good miner you need a computer with a powerful ATI graphics card (or even mulitple cards). A special software, the so called miner, calculates high-complex mathematical problems and creates Bitcoins. these “problems” result in a so called block – this block is currently worth 25 Bitcoins. A single computer would need months to solve a complete block. That’s why users have created so called mining pools. Miners unite in these pools and create an extrem high performance to solve the blocks even faster.
