4 Dimensions of health.
Dimensions of Health

The dimensions of health are interactions between different aspects of life, that balance to produce well being and satisfaction.

There are 4 dimensions of health that need to be balanced in order to be 'healthy'.
A major misconception about health is;

"If I am physically slim, and I feel like I’m physically healthy, and I eat all the correct foods. Then doesn't that mean I’m healthy?"

The answer is NO.

In order to be healthy: You're Physical, Social, Mental/Emotional and Spiritual Health all need to be well balanced.



1. Physical health:

- Wellness of body & absence of chronic pain/discomfort.
- Efficient functioning of body organs
- Level of fitness
- Nutritional status
- Degree of energy
- Body weight
- Resistance to disease
- Heredity & genetics make up physical potential but our physical health is determined by our lifestyle & behavior



2. Social health:

- Ability to interact with people (family, friends, others) in independent & appropriate way
- Form & maintain personal relationships
- Assert personal beliefs regardless of influence of peers & retain own personality & values.
 


3. Mental/Emotional health:

- Relates to how we feel about ourselves, how we express our emotions, how we adapt to range of demands.
- Self-concept (way we see ourselves), self-confidence & self-esteem.
- Feelings of accomplishment & success
- Ability to make decisions to resolve problems
- Capacity to function productively in society without exhibiting personally & socially disruptive behavior.



4. Spiritual health:

- Relates to sense of purpose & meaning in our life & feeling connected with others (family, peers, community) & society.
- Ideas, values, morals & aspirations influence spiritual health
- Relates to our ability to set realistic goals, appreciate needs & feelings of others, & have ambitions & aspirations.



-----------------------------------------------------

  • Our individual health is an interaction between these 4 dimensions.
  • Health is continually changing.
  • Breakdown in one aspect can impact on other aspects.

    Ways of being healthy

  • Individual is considered healthy when 4 dimensions of health are balanced.
  • Individual is primarily responsible for decisions they make about their own health.
  • Nutrition, physical activity, drug use & relationships have greatest impacts on health for young people.
  • Individual factors – lifestyle, behaviors, attitude, beliefs, hereditary potential.
  • Environmental factors – physical, economic, social & political conditions under which we live.
  • Health is dynamic, which means the aspects of health are always changing.
  • Not simply longevity (long life) or absence of disease, but also quality of life (adding life to years).
  • Epidemiology – study of disease & illness trends across large scale populations to identify broad risk factors.
  • Health promotion – process of enabling people to increase control over & to improve their health.
  • Health is relative to others & ourselves over time.
