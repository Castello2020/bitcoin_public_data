    What is Scouting?

    Scouting is the finest framework created for an active youth organization. Because of its strong emphasis on integrity, leadership and values, many churches, schools and other non-profit groups have adopted scouting as their official youth activity program.

    Their is a scouting program in nearly every country of the world and they mostly follow the original program developed by Sir Baden-Powell in 1910. MainTour covers many of these, but our major emphasis follows the Boy Scouts of America program.


      Cub Scouts - Adventures for Boys ages 7-10.
      Boy Scouts - Adventures for Boys ages 11-17.
      Venturers - Co-Ed Adventures for ages 14-20.

    Awesome Activities for Youth

    Scouting is the only organization that takes youth groups out regularly to pursue a wide range of wholesome recreational activities. These events are fun, exciting, educational, build self-esteem and create memories that last forever. Here are just a few:


      High Adventure - 1 & 2 Week Expeditions into backcountry wilderness either by hiking or river riding.
      Bikepacking - camping and traveling by bicycle.
      Sea Scouts - sailing ships on the big ocean, great lake or famous rivers.
      Deep Sea Adventures - Ocean fishing or kayaking trips
      Awesome Sleepovers - camp overnight on a famous warship, stadium ballfield or under the stars in a forest meadow.
      FREE & Discount Activity Prices - many attractions offer free or discounted admission to scouts and their families. (Type FREE or Discount in our search box!)
      National Geographic Tour - climb exotic rock formations, underground caverns and wildlife refuges.
      Service Hours - each year scouts around the world contribute over 20 million manhours of meaningful service.
      Legacy Project - each scout can design and build his own Eagle Service Project and leave a lasting legacy even before he becomes an adult.
      Real World Experience - scouting experience is valuable qualification to feature on a young man's application for employment or college.
      Leadership & Values - scouts learn valuable teamwork, leadership and communication skills.
      DARE Program - Active scouts are much more likely to avoid alcohol, tobacco and other dangerous drugs.
      Special Needs Youth - The individualized focus creates multiple activity opportunities to support special needs youth at their own pace.
      Everyone is a Winner - The emphasis in scouting is setting and accomplishing realistic fun goals.
