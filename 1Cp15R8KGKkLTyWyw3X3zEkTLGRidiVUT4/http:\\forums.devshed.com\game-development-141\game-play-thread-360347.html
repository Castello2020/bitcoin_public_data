    I like gameplay to be story based. I like having a certain emotional reason to perform tasks.

    I also really like humour in my games. A few good jokes will really sell a game to me.

    I dislike emphasis on wonderful technology over gameplay. If it isn't fun then I don't really care how much effort went into the physics engine or how realistic it is. I like having cars skid wildly, xplosions large and characters able to run and jump at superhuman speeds and distances

    Regarding the conversation AI, there was a game made for research called Facade, which can be downloaded from http://www.interactivestory.net/

    It's an interesting idea, and well worth checking out. Basically you talk to a couple who's marriage is on shaky ground. Their emotion and conversation etc. is controlled by AI.
