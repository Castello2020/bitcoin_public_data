    I wonder what country you're from and how much American Civics you've had because a state confederacy is pretty damm close to what the US federal government actually is.
    I am from the US and I have a BA in political science. The importance is very different, enough to cause a civil war. In a federal system the majority of judicial and legislative power rest in the unity of regions or states. In a confederal system the majority of judicial and legislative power and final decision is in the state government. The logic should follow that our ID card should represent our greatest established sovereignty, meaning that in a confederal system the state should issue each ID card but in a federal system the ID should be represented by the federal government. So my point was that it only makes sense for there to be one federal ID card, rather than a different drivers license for each state.
