    There are so many wireless hotspots popping up everywhere that, if you don't mind the risk of tapping into a free wifi connect, you can probably find them readily available. I drove across the country last year and noted many interstate turnpikes even had them at the rest areas. Also, about half the hotels now seem to have wifi access from the rooms, and it's a bit more secure usually than an unrestricted public zone (they give you a login code at the front desk). I did that, and got a 1-800 dial-up number from my ISP to fall back on and was able to connect and get online every day. I question the value of the more expensive options unless you'll be needing I-absolutely-must-get-on-this-very-minute access, in which case you might still be quite disappointed when you get to Montana and find you ain't got no signal ...
