So.. I finally started watching some shows. I watched the first episode of both Nisemonogatari and PoT 2... and I'm left with mixed feelings.

To begin with, I can really feel that I've become somewhat alienated from anime as compared to what I felt when I watched before. When it comes to Nisemonogatari.. I'm really not sure what to say. I feel brainfucked. I wouldn't say it has ecchi undertones but rather perverted undertones - the difference is that it's perverted in words and not really pictures. I don't like that aspect of it, but quite honestly, based on episode one, I wouldn't say it is innocent but rather harmless.

When it comes to the real heart of the story and anime... I'm conflicted. I like the art in the sense that it's unique and expresses itself well, but at the same time it's not something marvelous or anything. The same goes for the actual dialogues - it has some good moments and is quite alright at times.. but very far from overwhelming, closer to underwhelming if anything.

I really enjoyed Bakemonogatari and despite the flaws that I've seen already of Nisemonogatari, I think it will end up being quite a good show. I hope the dialogue picks up and that the perverted undertones lessens, but I feel the latter will not happen in the force I want.

Moving on to PoT2... I knew that the previous season was rather ridiculous and childish when I watched it some time ago. It over exaggerated things a lot to give on example.. but despite that, I ended up having quite a good time. I love the moments when characters overcome their limits and do their best etc - just like I enjoy watching exciting matches, and there were quite a few of those. So, with that being said, the first season could quite simply be summed up as this: It's enjoyable and exciting, but it's quite childish with it's over exaggerated nonsense and special moves.

This sequel or season, whatever you want to call it, feels just like that. Over the top, childish, but do I want to watch more and do I like it? Yes. It's not something I would pay a hundred bucks to watch or anything like that, but overall.. it's quite alright. It's the kind of series I would love and do love to marathon.


I give both of these series a 7/10 as it is now. They're a far cry from a lot of series right now, and frankly when looking at series like Chihayafuru and comparing it to.. say, PoT2.. the latter feels extremely underwhelming.


To sum up all the things I've said so far: I'm disappointed that Nisemonogatari's first episode had perverted undertones, but it was harmless and overall I'd say the episode was decent enough. I expect more from the series in the coming episodes though, especially from the dialogue. PoT2 feels just like the first series - it's enjoyable and exciting at times, but it's full of clichés and childish over exaggerations such as completely unrealistic moves, to name one thing. Despite its obvious flaws however, I like these kinds of series somewhat - especially since they make for a good series to marathon.
