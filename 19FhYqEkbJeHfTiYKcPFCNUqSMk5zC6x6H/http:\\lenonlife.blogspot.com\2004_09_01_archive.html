Monday, September 20, 2004

it's been a long long time

It has been a long time since I have written anything here... Here is a summary of what has been going on:

Friday:
worked all day then went home and hung out with Hamish for a while. Nothing really other than that.

Saturday:
it was awesome weather in the morning and I sat out in the backyard and read a book. Enjoyed the sun and warm weather and just relaxed. In the afternoon I made some roll-up taco things for Jeremy and Brooke's potluck dinner. Went over to Jeremy's and met Lauren and decided to do some hiking on Sunday with her. Lauren is an American from California. Pretty cool chick... Hit up the potluck dinner in the evening and had a good time.

Sunday:
Lauren and I left to head up to the Billy Goat Track near the Pinnacles in the morning. A ways into the track we stopped by a river and decided that we would walk up the river and meet back up with the trail farther up. Yeah... we got really wet, then thought it would be a good idea to walk through the bush back to the track. 45 minutes through a swamp full of razor grass and we got back to the track bloody and tired. It was rather amusing though. We ended up sitting on some lookout overlooking the Pinnacles and east towards Waihi. Beautiful spot. Lauren made monkey calls and scared some random kids hiking the track. After the hike we went to some hotpools and had a soak. Came back to Hamilton and she cooked me dinner. I talked to James and we decided to skip work on Monday and go down to the mountain and do some skiing. We took off at 9pm and got to Ohakune at about midnight.

Monday:
In the morning we woke up and had some breakfast at the Rimu Lodge (where we stayed) and then headed up the mountain. The Giant Lift and the High Flyer were not operating because they were doing avalanche control on the upper mountain. It was about noon before it opened and everybody just stood in line and had a massive snowball fight. Amusing, but annoying. We had a good afternoon of skiing on dry powder. Pictures are coming soon, but I accidently left my camera at home this morning.

Tuesday:
I am at work now. I guess there was a bit of commotion about James, David, and I taking off yesterday, but I am not worried about it until I am told directly. My immediate boss said what I did was ok, so I am sticking to that.

Monday, September 06, 2004

Tired and sore - rewards for powder

On Friday night after work I left and drove down to Turangi to stay at my co-worker's timeshare that he had for the week. Saturday morning we got up and headed off to Turoa because the snow was looking better there than at Whakapapa. We had an excellent day. Plenty of loose snow and some powder to be found. The clouds were pretty low so we skiied above them all day and it was clear as. That night we went back to Turangi and ate dinner at Valentino's then had a spa and crashed out.

Sunday morning the weather looked terrible so we took our time and cooked a nice breakfast. We hung out for a while and then Darran and I went to Taupo for the day. David stayed behind and rested up. James and Geoff showed up that evening.

Monday morning they had received fresh snow at Whakapapa and Turoa, but Whakapapa was saying it was hard off trail and Turoa said soft. We went to Turoa again and it rocked! A lot of fresh snow and nobody there. The sun was shining and we had a lot of fun kickin around in the powpow. Rode lots of steep terrain and jumped off some cliffs.
