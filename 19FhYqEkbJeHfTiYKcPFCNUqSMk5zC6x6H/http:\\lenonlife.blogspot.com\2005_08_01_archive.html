bike bike bike work work work bike bike bike

Current music: The Kinison - You'll Never Guess Who Died

Last night Elena and I went to a jazz concert at Samana. It was fun, but she was tired so we left after about an hour.

This morning I had plans with Dan to go mountain biking. We decided to ride Mill Creek Road up to Joint Point. I thought that sounded great because the last time I had tried to do that ride it started lightning and hailing on me and I had to turn around.

Just before the bottom of the South 6 trail Jeff met up with us. He had already been riding somewhere on Vail. He rode with us up to Joint Point and they both wanted to do more. I was so tired at that point, but agreed to go on. We rode up to the top and then down the service roads all the way to PHQ where we took the Grand Traverse over to Eagle's Nest and the Liondown to Gitalong Road back to the base. Before we got to PHQ I had fallen twice just from exhaustion. On the Grand Traverse I fell again. Not sure if this time it was a rock or what, but my front wheel is taco'd a little bit. My back brake is all screwed up now too.

Tomorrow there will be no riding. I need to recover before Jeff and I hike Quandry on Friday. I think I will work on my bike tomorrow and see if I can get the front wheel true again. I think I just need to adjust the pads on the rear brake. I hope that is all...

Monday, August 29, 2005

work sucks lately

Current music: The Get Up Kids - Mass. Pike

I am sick of the math. I am sick of not understanding what I am doing. That is pretty much how I feel about work lately.

Oh well. Yesterday was cool. Megan and I took Scout for another bike ride then Ryan and I had lunch at the Vail Farmer's Market. Elena met up with us and we went for a hike up to Booth Falls.

Good hike, but I really need to prepare myself for the ass-kicking I am gonna get when Jeff and I do Quandry this weekend. My first Colorado 14er and I can't wait.

Today I slacked around and read a book. Went for a quick 4 mile bike ride. Now I am working. Back to it I guess...

Saturday, August 27, 2005

big day for the scout

Current music: The Flaming Lips - Fight Test

Yesterday was a fun day. Got up early and did some work, then took and break and went for a mountain bike ride with Megan and Scout...


I worked for a while more after the ride then went up the gondola to FAC (Friday Afternoon Club) at Eagle's Nest with Jeff, Nick, Ryan, and Elena. Ryan, Elena, and I met their realtor, her boyfriend, and Megan out for dinner after that at Blue Tiger. Oh... the sushi was awesome.

After dinner I went over to Ryan and Elena's new place and checked it out. Cool place. Then we went out to Samana and hung out there the rest of the night.

This morning Jeff and I went over to Ryan and Elena's new place, then went with them to get some lunch at Red Lion. They are up checking out Game Creek for their wedding. I am just gonna be big chillin' and reading a book for a while then probably hanging out with them later.

What a great life up here in the Colorado high country...

Thursday, August 25, 2005

good day so far

Current music: Sublime - Santeria

I woke up this morning and had an awesome email from Marjo, so I called her. First time I have talked to her since I have been back in the States.

I went for a bike ride up Vail Mountain. It started raining just before I got to Mid-Vail so I pedalled a little harder and took shelter there. After about 10 minutes the blew over so I rode the rest of the way up to Eagle's Nest and took Liondown back to the village.

When I got home I read a little more of Tokyo by Mo Hayder. Excellent book so far. Hard to put it down. I am glad Johanna recommended it to me in Fiji.

Now it is time to do some work. I have a lot to do in the next month, so I better get cracking like the lightning happening outside right now.

Tuesday, August 23, 2005

Finally... Sky Chutes

Current music: Modest Mouse - Doin' the Cockroach

Hulsey and I have wanted to hike the Sky Chutes out of Copper for several months now. We finally did it today. After the long hike my body is tired. Much of the hike is over 12,000 feet (3636m). After coming back to a lower elevation Jeff made a comment about feeling like we had been walking on the moon. The highest elevation we reached was at the top of Peak 8 of the Tenmile Range, 12,987 feet (3935m).

Wednesday, August 17, 2005

work will never end this week

Current music: The Sounds - Seven Days a Week

Denver was fun yesterday. Went out to eat at the Cheesecake Factory and Dan met us there and we went to the Rockies game. The Rockies lost, but they did make a run for it at the end.

Dan and I had to drive back to Vail after the game, but I-70 is closed due to a large rock slide near Idaho Springs. We took CO-285 to SH-9 to get back here. Long drive and we got back at about 1:30am. Took about 2.5 hours.

This morning I slept in then rode into town to get some chain lube for my bikes. Washed and worked on both bikes. Both of them have been shifting a little funny lately. The Bianchi was really bad and I had to adjust the cable, but the Specialized just needed a little barrel-adjuster adjusted.

Hulsey's parents got here today and I think they are going to stop by my place a little later. It will be good to see them.

Well, back to work since I really didn't do anything the past two days. I will need to bust my ass the rest of the week and probably the weekend too...

In the morning it was beautiful. We did a hike up to Emerald Lake:


and up to the top of the mountain next to the Alpine Visitor's Center:

When we got back here to ate lunch at La Cantina and then my dad and I went to the Sonnenalp Golf Club with Dan and Spencer to play a round. That is very hard course.

Tomorrow I am heading down to Denver with my parents and going to a Rockies game with them. Dan will meet up with us to go to the game too after he takes his girlfriend to the airport.
