Friday, July 29, 2005

work work working

Got up early this morning and started working. I had to mail some things in NZ to get reimbursed for my Finland trip. Finally got that taken care of, now I just need the money.

Didn't end up playing disc golf with Dan, but this afternoon I might play with Jeff up on top of Vail. After that I am heading to the Ultra 100 registration dinner with Jeff and Jeff in the evening. Can't complain about a free dinner.

Working at Jeff's has been pretty sweet even though I feel like a jerk being here all the time. I have been taking Tasha out for a 2 mile run every time she gets too active. That gets her all paid out then she goes to sleep for a while.

Thursday, July 28, 2005

Buffehr Creek

This morning Jeff's friend Jeff Lapp and I were supposed to ride the Buffehr Creek loop, but we ended up waiting for each other at different locations and never met up. We rode the loop separately, but still had a great time. I love that ride. The uphill climb is pretty tough and steep, but the downhill is so much fun. It is fast, gentle turns. It is so fast that I had to stop about half way down and shake my hands out from squeezing the brakes so much. Jeff Lapp said his rotors were so hot he couldn't touch them.

I am working now at Jeff's for the rest of the evening. Good times.

Friday, July 22, 2005

Why do I do this to myself?

Yesterday Sean took off for Deluge Lake. He camped in the cabin by the lake. This morning Kris and I hiked up to the lake to meet up with him. It took us 4 hours to get there and when we finally got there we were so cooked. Riding over 30 miles yesterday was bad enough, then adding this steep climb to it... ugh.

So, we get there and look around and can't find Sean anywhere. He wasn't in the cabin and there was no sign of him. After hanging out for a while we decided to leave and hope that Sean had gone down and we missed him or he took a different trail or something. Just as we were leaving we heard him start yelling from way up one of the peaks. He had been sitting on top of the Gore Range. I can't believe he climbed all the way up there in Birkenstocks.

After our hike we got some dinner at Pazzo's and now I am doing some work before Ryan shows up.

Thursday, July 21, 2005

Longest bike ride...ever

Current music: Cake - Love You Madly

Kris and I got up this morning with intentions of doing a bike ride. When we got to the hotel to pick up a bike from Jeff he suggested that we take a ride with the activities van up to the top of Vail Pass and ride over Shrine Pass. So we did...

Shrine Pass was amazing. The ride was a lot of fun and the views were spectacular. We rode all the way down to Red Cliff then had to climb up Battle Mountain Pass and ride down Route 24 to Minturn. At that point Kris and I were tired. We still had to ride all the way up from there to Vail.

The entire ride was a bit over 30 miles and took us about 3 hours of riding time. Kris is sleeping right now at my place. He and Jeff are going on some "Hummer and Martini" tour/party thing tonight with the Sonnenalp. I have to work...

Wednesday, July 20, 2005

Back to Vail

Current music: Air - Cherry Blossom Girl

Not posting for almost a week... That is unlike me, but I have made it back to Vail now. It feels good to be back. So, since I last posted I left Finland. I was sad to leave Marjo, but glad to get back to New York to see the family.

After multiple delayed flights and a night in a hotel in Copenhagen I made it home at about midnight on the 15th. My grandparents came in from Florida on the 16th in the evening. My cousin, Jaime, had her high school graduation party on Sunday. My parents picked up Sean to come to it. I was glad he could make it. He has been living with his 20 year old girlfriend who has 3 kids. He lost his job and is really struggling. I asked him to drive back to Colorado with Kris and I so he could have a bit of a break and clear his head and make a decision. He had told my mom that he didn't know what he wanted to do with his life. I don't think where he is now is great for him. He said he would come, but then his girlfriend talked him out of it when he got home that night.

Monday morning Kris and I went to the airport and picked up the rental car. When we got back to my parents' house Sean called and was saying he decided not to go. My mom talked to him for a while and right when Kris and I were getting in the car to start the journey my mom said Sean was coming with us. Kris and I repacked the car to make room for Sean and then I drove to Warsaw and picked him up.

We left Buffalo at about 2pm after eating Mighty Taco with Jeff. The drive went well and we made it to Chicago to see Dan Lindsey and his girlfriend at 10pm. We had some dinner with them at a deep dish pizza place then hit the road again.

Kris and I traded driving the rest of the way and made it to Denver at 4pm the next day. Ryan and one of his friends met us out for a drink then we drove the rest of the way to Vail. Of course, the first thing we did was meet Dan and Brienne at La Cantina for dinner... The best mexican as usual.

This morning Kris and I took the car to the Eagle airport then we came back and got Sean to do some mountain biking. We got some bikes for the guys and hit the trails. We rode the Vail Village Loop Trail up to the base of Chair 10 then took South 6 down. They were pretty paid. I think they are at home sleeping right now. I came down to Jeff's because the internet at my place isn't working again. Damn Comcast...

Goodbye Finland

We went to a concert, the Rotuaari Piknik. Some good Finnish bands played and I didn't know any of the words, but it was still fun. After the concert we rode our bikes for a little while down near the water and just hung out until late into the night. Goodbye to a face like this really sucks...

Wednesday, July 13, 2005

Poets shmoets...

Finland rocks in the summer. Since the sun doesn't really go down it stays warm all night. Last night Marjo and I went out to a really nice restaurant on the water for dinner. I had some really good lamb and she had an ENTIRE chicken! After dinner we called Hulsey so he could hear her Finnish accent and hear her speak Finnish. Pretty funny. Later in the night we went out to a pizzaria and met up with her cousin and her cousin's friend. It was a good night.

I tried to change my flight so that I fly to Copenhagen in the morning on Friday then back too hte US in the afternoon, but there are no flights available for Friday morning to Copenhagen. I will have to leave here tomorrow afternoon and head to Copenhagen, then spend the night there and fly to the US on Friday afternoon. Kinda sucks, but I will get another half day of work done, so that is good.

Well, back to the debugging...

Just got back from an amazing night with Marjo. We rode down into the city sqaure and had dinner, then rode our bikes all around the city. We went to some island called some word which is similar to the Finnish word for penis.

Anyway, we found a nice place on the river where there are fountains and stayed there until the mosquitoes carried us away. She hates photos, but I managed to get one almost decent one of her almost smiling:


Finally we rode back to the hotel and now she is riding home. Time for bed for me because I have to be up in 5 hours to catch the taxi to work. Hopefully we will see each other again tomorrow night before I leave on Tuesday.

Ahhh... Scandinavian girls

Yesterday I got a bike and rode all around. The women here are awesome. I always heard that Scandinavian women are beautiful and you see the Swedish women in magazines and TV, but whoa. They are amazing. Anyway, I met this really cute girl at the beach named Marjo. We rode our bikes in to town together and hung out at a bar all night until closing. I met some of her friends. Everybody was key...

Today Marjo and I have been texting all day. I was up at the Arctic Circle, but we made plans to get dinner when I got back to Oulu. She is riding her bike here right now, then we are going out. I don't think of Oulu as a place I would like to live, but meeting her makes me want to stay here past Tuesday for sure.

Friday, July 08, 2005

It's been such a long long time

Chris' party in Peconic, Long Island was a blast. A little more low key than years past. Jesse Shoemaker showed up and it was awesome to see that face. We got some good sunsets at the beach and had good times together:




After that 3 days of party, Tor and I drove to Syracuse. My parents picked me up there and I went home for 2 days. I thought I would be staying there all week, but work decided I needed to visit a customer in Oulu, Finland. I flew out on the 6th in the morning.

Now I am in Finland and I have been here for two nights. I have to stay for another few days, but its cool. I am having fun here. The first night, since the sun never sets, I woke up about 2am and went out for a walk around the city. Believe it or not, but I took these shots at about 2:40am:




Yesterday, Jouko and his girlfriend took me on a boat cruise in the sea in the evening. It is amazing that the sunsets can last for so long. The entire time we were on the cruise it was setting (4 hrs) and it still hadn't set when I went to bed at 11:30pm.
