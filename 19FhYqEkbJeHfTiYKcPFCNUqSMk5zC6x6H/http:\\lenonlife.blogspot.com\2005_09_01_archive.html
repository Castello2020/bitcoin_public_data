part 2

Marjo got back to me this afternoon. I was so stoked. After work I went over to her new apartment and hung out there for a while until she had to go to a party. It was so good to see her, but it was kinda awkward too. We will see what happens with that boyfriend of hers...

Back in Finland

So, here I am back in Finland. I thought that if I came back here that I would see Marjo right away, but that is not the case. She is back with her old boyfriend and I don't know when I will see her at all. She says Saturday, but so far she is not returning my texts. We will see...

On a brighter note, I forgot how unbelieveable looking the girls are here. All of them. Last time, since I met Marjo the second day I was here, I didn't really pay as much attention to other girls. Well, let me tell you, it is amazing. I will just have to find a different one ;)

When I landed in Copenhagen yesterday, our deplaning music was a song by the Kings of Convenience. I was so stoked. The Rasmus is playing here in Oulu on Sunday night and I will be there.

Time to get to work and fix some problems for these guys...

Rochester time

I am working today and probably will put in a few hours at some point tomorrow as well. Sunday I think I will head to Buffalo to visit some friends.

Wednesday, September 07, 2005

city boy, yes i am

Current music: Death Cab For Cutie - Soul Meets Body

So after quite a few years of trying to figure something out, I think I finally have. I love city life. This however conficts with my love of snowboarding since snowboarding in cities usually sucks and the concrete and asphalt ruin boards quickly.

I love the concerts.
I love the indie movie theatres.
I love walking around the city.
I love having tons of people.
I love the public transportation.
I love city biking.
I love the bookstores, CD stores, and magazine shops.
I love the ghetto bowling alleys.
I love the metropolitan women.
I love cities.

So, I have done 4 years in Plattsburgh (too small), then almost 2 in Buffalo (good size), then a year in Hamilton, NZ (too small), and now I am in Vail (way too small). I am gonna have to make the move after this snowboard season to a city... I just can't handle it anymore. Not that I don't love living in Vail. I do love this place. This is my favorite place I have ever lived, but there are too many things I miss...
