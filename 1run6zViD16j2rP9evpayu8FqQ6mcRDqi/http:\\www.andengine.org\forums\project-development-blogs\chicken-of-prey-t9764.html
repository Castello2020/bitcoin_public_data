[BETA] Chicken Of Prey

by JensL » Fri Nov 02, 2012 2:25 pm

Hi everyone,

i proudly invite all of you to the open beta of my first game Chicken Of Prey :
https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.jel.cop.beta

After humanity used up all resources of the earth they left the planet. A new age dawned for the animals but the peace did not last long.
Meet Pete “Maveregg”. A hot shot chicken pilot ready to protect his home against the attacking ducks.



https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.jel.cop.beta

The beta contains the intro and 7 levels of the future release. I will soon add a debug level to show you all the features and enemies.
I plan to release a free version with ~10 levels and a pay-version with ~30 levels and no ads.

I am very interested in your honest opinion about (Dont suspend your judgement, i can take it):
Bugs / Crashes
Gameplay
Fun factor
Graphics
Performance(!)
Sound

One last request. Please play with an active internet connection. I use flurry to send error and performance reports right back to me (of course only with an active internet connection).

i hope you have fun with my little game.
Jens

Re: [BETA] Chicken Of Prey

by joaquinrok » Sat Jan 05, 2013 7:10 am

Awsome project!

Tried it on a Motorola Defy, it was pretty good, actually much harder that what it looked like.

One gameplay suggestion:
- Being able to go free fall, just stop "flying" and just fall when not touching the screen, that way it would feel more like a bird flying, and not so much as a spaceship ignoring gravity. Also, I felt there should be more of a "constraint" when going directly upwards, it just keeps going up until it hits the roof, and feels pretty unrealistic, maybe it could stop ascending and start a descend, like planes do in most games. (not like there's that much air space when playing on a small phone, but its an idea)

- The pace of the game also tends to get a bit slow, its hard to aim the targets so you tend to keep flying a lot and turning around. Of course, after some practice, that gets better, but the first levels get pretty hard as you get used to the controls.

Apart from that, great graphics, music is outstanding, and the birds/animals/pos-apocalyptic "theme" is great
Congratulations for the work!
