Random Musings - February 2012

Random Musings of the Month

Happy Feb 29th!
Well the awards season is over without much surprise, and I am pleased that A Separation won for Best Foreign Language film. For the first time since 2003 I agree with the Academy's choice.

It was also nice to see a French film dominate the major awards, even though I wasn't as impressed with it as much as most. However, the ceremony as always, was way too long and boring. I wish they would cut the unfunny fluff and let the winners have more time at the podium. Also why didn't they perform the nominated songs? Terrible decision.

The funniest line of the weekend was delivered on Friday by Antoine de Caunes, host of the César Awards, which was otherwise just as dull as the Oscars were. Just before the presentation of the in memoriam montage he quipped: "This year, we lost a giant of cinema. Megaupload."

For some quick blog news, I took a slight dip in page views compared to January, but still managed to pass the 250K mark. With some help from an increased interest in my coverage of the foreign language nominees, and also being selected as Lamb of the Month.

I've also been toying with the idea of adding a ranked blog roll widget to my sidebar. Similar to the LAMB's Leaderboard, to reward the blogs who send me the most referral traffic, except this one will be automatically updated. What do you guys think?

I caught up with quite a few 2011 releases this month, but there are still a bunch more I want to see, hopefully I can get to them in soon. In March, I'll also be continuing with my Czech New Wave series which I'm about half way through; and I've begun to source some films for my next major spotlight, I'm not yet ready to reveal what it will be, but the idea was inspired by this year's Berlinale.

I'm the LAMB of the Month!


Today I'm taking a break from my usual posting schedule to announce something that is quite exciting and a honor for me to be a part of. Just this past weekend, I was selected by Joel Burman—a fellow blogger and film producer who is also the recently appointed Shepherd of the LAMB (Large Association of Movie Blogs)—as the very first recipient of the LAMB of the Month Award, in recognition of some of my recent contributions to this great blogging community.

For those who have never heard of the LAMB, its the largest directory of movie blogs on the internet, open to any movie blog that's at least 3 months old, with approximately 1200 current members—though as I've recently discovered by paying a visit to almost every single one, sadly a number of them are now dead or dormant blogs—first created by Dylan from the blog Man I Love Films. I feel greatly honored for having been singled out of this large group of fine bloggers and chosen as January's LAMB of the Month.

Thank you, Joel!

62nd Berlin International Film Festival (2012)

In just a few short days, the 62nd Berlin International Film Festival (Internationale Filmfestspiele Berlin) or more commonly referred to as The Berlinale, one of the three major annual film festivals, opens with what promises to be another amazing lineup of films from all over the world.

First held in 1951, the festival has grown to be the largest publicly attended film festival in the world. According to their website there were 485,000 visitors and 300,000 tickets sold at last year's festival.

Films are screened in several categories, including the flagship Main Competition Programme, where this year 18 films will vie for the coveted Golden and Silver Bear prizes awarded by the International Jury. There's also the Panorama section, which focuses on art-house and auteur films from around the globe; the Forum section, covering avant garde, experimental, political minded, and other daring features and documentaries alike;  and the Generation programme, which is dedicated to films for children and young people. With two sections titled Generation Kplus for younger audiences and Generation 14plus focusing on teens and above.  In addition to those, there are special screenings of classics and important films, retrospectives, and homages to internationally renown film personalities, this year's Homage is dedicated to American actress Meryl Streep, and a special screening of her most recent film The Iron Lady will be held at the famed Berlinale Palast.
